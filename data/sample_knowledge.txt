The payments service is owned by the platform team. It depends on the billing service for invoice generation, and the billing service depends on the pricing engine for rate calculations.


In March, the platform team migrated the payments service database from MySQL to Postgres. This migration was driven by scaling issues encountered during the previous quarter.


The support team escalates payments-related tickets to the platform team when they involve failed transactions. The average response time for these escalations is four hours.


The enterprise plan includes dedicated infrastructure and a 99.9% uptime SLA. The scale plan does not include dedicated infrastructure but does include priority support.


Last quarter, an incident in the payments service was traced back to a configuration change in the billing service deployed the same week. The incident was resolved by rolling back that configuration change.


Access to the payments service requires approval from the platform team lead. New engineers get read-only access by default and must request elevated access separately.
